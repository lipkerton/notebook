How can I describe a relatively new situation which is now familiar or usual? #flashcard 
By using **be used to**:
> After moving to the new city, I had a hard time adjusting to the fast-paced lifestyle, but now, after a few months, I **am used to** the hustle and bustle of the city and the long commute to work.

How can I talk about a new situation which is becoming familiar or usual? #flashcard 
By using **get used to**:
> When I first started playing the guitar, my fingers hurt a lot because I wasn't used to pressing the strings down, but now I have been practising for a few weeks and I am **getting used to** it.

How can I describe a past habit or situation that no longer exists in the present? #flashcard 
By using **used to**:
> I **used to** work long hours but now I work a regular 9-to-5 job.
<!--ID: 1755929153175-->


How can I talk about repeated actions in past or about past habits? #flashcard 
I can use **would**:
> Every morning, she **would** take her dog for a walk in the park.
