## Notes
- Автор будет аргументировать в пользу иного подхода к рассмотрению креативности у ИИ. Традиционный подход заключается в отрицании агентности у ИИ; на основании отрицания агентности выдвигается заключение об отсутствии у ИИ способности к творчеству. Автор предлагает концепцию "минимальной агентности", которую можно было бы использовать для аргументации агентности ИИ.
- Аргумент агентности - это аргумент построенный в пользу самостоятельности ИИ, т.е. в пользу его способностей к творчеству на основе этой самостоятельности.
- Не ясно могут ли программы быть креативными, потому что не ясно могут ли они быть агентами. [[Creativity, Agency, and AI#^aa39e1]]
- Возможность креативности ИИ отвергается на основании отсутсвия у машин агентности, потому что под агентностью имеется в виду способность к интенциональному действию. [[Creativity, Agency, and AI#^fef3b1]]]
- ИИ не способен мыслить > не способен на производство ментальных состояний > не способен совершать действия интенционально > не способен творить.
- Складывается цепочка из пяти премисс: [[Creativity, Agency, and AI#^fc1b19]]
- Против первой премисы: не все виды креативности требуют агентности (например, "дарвиновский" подход к креативности или поход к креативности Маргарет Боден). [[Creativity, Agency, and AI#^cf6303]]
- Против четвертой премисы: связь между ментальными состояниями и интенциональностью не ясна - существуют походы, которые относят интенциональность не к ментальным состояниям. [[Creativity, Agency, and AI#^30c35f]]
- Автор хочет сфокусироваться в первую очередь на второй и первой премисе. Для этого он анализирует работу Gaut, которая посвящена доказательству того, что креативность требует наличия агентности. 
- Основной аргумент автора против позиции Гаута исходит из того, что Гаут в своей работе утверждает, что креативность может быть действием, а может и не быть, точно так же как она может быть телеологическим действием (т.е. действием с определенной целью), а может и не быть. Из этого не совсем понятно почему агентность является необходимой для креативного процесса, если она не вытекает с однозначностью из действия или из вдохновения. [[Creativity, Agency, and AI#^d2a16c]]
- Остается ли в такой теории место для не-человеческой креативности? В определениях агентности Гаута нет четкого указания на то, что агентность может быть присвоена только человеку. [[Creativity, Agency, and AI#^d0a2db]]
- Однако Гаут говорит в некоторых местах о том, что агентом может быть и не-человеческая сущность. [[Creativity, Agency, and AI#^1d10c9]]
- Стокс говорит, что сущность нуждается хотя бы в "процессоре", которому мы можем присвоить агентность на основании ответсвенности. Без ответственности не может быть и креативности. [[Creativity, Agency, and AI#^58d0f7]] 
- Произведение является креативным только если оно зависит от агентности агента. [[Creativity, Agency, and AI#^af9a5d]] 
- Агентность при этом не может осуществляться без действия. [[Creativity, Agency, and AI#^2e90f3]]
- "не случайность" - минимальное требование, чтобы считать что-то креативным. [[Creativity, Agency, and AI#^d4fbf8]]
- Напротив, Гаут считает, что действие не является необходимым для осуществления креативного процесса. Позднее он вообще говорит о том, что креативность является свойством и предрасположенностью агентов. [[Creativity, Agency, and AI#^242593]]

## Quotes
> We can formulate an argument against AI creativity from agency. By some accounts, creativity requires agency, and agency is, many think, not possible for AI. (стр. 1)

> Intentional action is thought to require mental states, a severe challenge for machine intelligence. (стр. 1)

> This paper lays out the argument against the possibility of creativity in artificial intelligence (AI) from agency (what I refer to as the ‘agency argument’). (стр. 1)

> Agency is, it is often argued, not possible for AI. Agency therefore may seem an easy route for those who wish to deny the possibility of AI creativity. (стр. 1)

> I will examine the first two of these premises, arguing that anyone wishing to put forward the agency argument ought to defend against an alternative understanding of agency as required for creativity. (стр. 1)

> The reason it isn’t clear that these programs are really creative is because it isn’t clear that they are really agents. It all boils down to the tricky question of what kind of agency is required for creativity and whether computers can be agents in that way. (Stokes & Paul, 2021) (стр. 2)
^aa39e1

> Agency is typically defined as requiring the capacity for intentional action. (стр. 2)
^fef3b1

> According to this view, a being has the capacity to exercise agency just in case it has the capacity to act intentionally, and the exercise of agency consists in the performance of intentional actions and, in many cases, in the performance of unintentional actions (that derive from the performance of intentional actions). (Schlosser, 2019) (стр. 2)

> If agency is a requirement of creativity, and is comprised of the mental state of intention, then this could present a significant roadblock for artificial creativity and, indeed, for advancements in AI more generally. The widely held position today is that AI is not capable of mental states such as intentions. This is because AI is typically considered to be incapable of having mind. (стр. 2)

> We could then formulate the agency argument against AI creativity as follows:
> P1 Creativity requires agency
> P2 Agency requires the capacity for intentional action
> P3 Intentional actions require intentions 
> P4 Intentions require mental states
> P5 AI cannot have mental states
> C Therefore, AI cannot be creative (стр. 3)
^fc1b19

> [against P1] Not all accounts of creativity require agency. For example, the Darwinian account of creativity (Simonton, 1999) does not require agency, (see Helliwell, 2021 for discussion in relation to AI), and neither does Margaret Boden’s account (2018). (стр. 3)
^cf6303

> [against P4] Not all accounts of creativity require agency. For example, the Darwinian account of creativity (Simonton, 1999) does not require agency, (see Helliwell, 2021 for discussion in relation to AI), and neither does Margaret Boden’s account (2018). (стр. 3)
^30c35f

> [against P5] Whilst many find this position convincing, many have also argued (particularly contra Searle, 1980) that AI could indeed have mental states (see Cole, 2023 for a summary). (стр. 3)

> For Gaut, in order to be creative, one must be an agent. (стр. 4)

> [Gaut] But the tree is not acting at all, since it lacks desires, beliefs and other intentional states; so a fortiori it cannot be acting creatively. Creativity is a property of agents, not of mere things or plants (see also Carruthers, The Architecture of the Mind, ch. 5; Stokes). (Gaut, 2010, p. 1040) (стр. 4)

> Creativity, I have argued, is a property of an agent with certain capacities. It does not follow from this that all creative processes (sequences of events) are actions, only that such processes must occur in agents. (Gaut, 2010, p. 104) (стр. 5)

> As Gaut acknowledges, this leads to an objection: if creative processes are not necessarily actions, then why does creativity necessitate agency? (стр. 5)

> So, for Gaut, creative process can be actions but are not necessarily actions and, similarly, are not necessarily teleological, but also can be. After all this, what remains unclear is why Gaut sees agency as necessary as it does not stem from action and it does not stem from inspiration (i.e., anti-teleology). (стр. 5)
^d2a16c

> Where does this leave nonhuman creativity? Gaut does not necessarily preclude nonhumans from creativity providing that they are agents. As he explains, “creativity ... is a particular exercise of agency. As such it is open to agents, whether human or not, that have the requisite capacities.” (Gaut 2010, p. 1041). Gaut allows that the agents in question may, in fact, not be human.
^d0a2db

> It is therefore not clear who or what he means to let in with the claim that creativity is “open to agents, whether human or not”. We might assume that he is merely leaving the door open for nonhuman agents (or other descendants of great apes per Carruthers) to be creative if it transpires that they can indeed have the appropriate capacities. This may well include computational systems. (стр. 6)
^1d10c9

> Stokes claims that, at minimum, a creative process needs a “processor” with agency. This agent must be “responsible”; without a responsible agent, we cannot attribute creativity (Stokes, 2008, p. 116). (стр. 6)
^58d0f7

> Creative artworks are things that are done and made, and for which we praise their makers. The processes that generate them involve intentional agency and it is this process, at least in part, for which we praise the agent. This implies that the process must depend in some non-trivial way upon that agency. We do not appropriately praise (any more than we blame) agents for processes out of their control. We capture this intuition with a simple condition on creativity; call it the agency condition. Some F is creative only if F counterfactually depends upon the agency of an agent A. (Stokes, 2008, p. 116) (стр. 6)
^af9a5d

> So, according to Stokes, not only does creativity require the creator to be an agent, but the creative process must also depend non-trivially on the agency of that agent. (стр. 6)

> For Stokes, the requirement for agency then stems from a requirement that creators ought to be held responsible for their creations if they are, in fact, creative. (стр. 7)

> These features are those that Gaut gathers under the condition of “flair”. The first condition here, that creative actions are not accidental, aligns with the praiseworthiness or blameworthiness that Stokes highlights as key for creativity. (стр. 7)

> Stokes implies that creativity is a kind of action: “Agency involves action. Creative things, whatever else is true of them, are things we do as agents” (Stokes, 2011, p. 660). (стр. 7)
^2e90f3

> This leads Stokes to the necessary condition of agency: “an x is minimally creative only if x is the non-accidental result of agency.” (Stokes, 2011, p. 660). Stokes intends this condition to select only actions and consequences that non-accidentally result from agents. (стр. 7)
^d4fbf8

> Gaut, on the other hand, is not wedded to the claim that creative things are actions, or even doings. Gaut states that creative processes (which may include actions) must be done by agents with the right capacities. Gaut has referred to creativity as both a property of agents and, in a later paper, as a disposition of agents (Gaut 2018, pp. 132-133). (стр. 8)
^242593
