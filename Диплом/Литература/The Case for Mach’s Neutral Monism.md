## Quotes

> There are now two main traditions in the literature, one tying Mach to positivism and the other to neutral monism. In this paper, I will defend the neutral monist tradition and show that it is actually a form of scientific realism, not positivism. (стр. 1)

> Neutral monism holds that objects and the human mind are both concatenations of neutral elements (e) neither exclusively mental nor physical in nature. (стр. 2)

> These elements are transient event particulars that never recur. (стр. 2)

> In addition to elements which obey both mental and physical variations (e/s), there are also elements that make up the rest of the physical world, other minds, animal sensations, etc. (стр. 2)

> These are found by causal continuity with our experienced sensations, by extending the functions we find in perception to complete unobserved objects “in thought” and by adding extra (hinzugedachte) elements. (стр. 2)
