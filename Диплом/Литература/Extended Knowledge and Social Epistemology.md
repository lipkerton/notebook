## Notes
- С самого начала автор показывает две группы. Между этими группами разногласия по поводу сущности социальной эпистемологии:
	- epistemology should be pursued strictly from within the perspective of individualistic analytic epistemology.
	- second camp holds that social epistemology is an interdisciplinary field that should be given priority over traditional analytic epistemology, with the specific aim of radically transforming the latter to fit the results and methodology of the former.
- Что такое социальная эпистемология?
	- Цель этого поля  -  исследовать получение и производства знания. [[Extended Knowledge and Social Epistemology#^7440eb|цитата]]
- История со всей социальной эпистемологией начинается с парня по имени Голдман, который захотел расширить традиционное индивидуалистическое представление в эпистемологии. Вместо него он предложил несколько подходов и самые популярные из них - preservationist social epistemology и expansionist social epistemology. Основная проблема, которую решают все эти подходы - "тестирование" традиционных индивидуалистических принципов в эпистемологии, через демонстрацию того, что эпистемология имеет еще и социальный аспект. Проблема Голдмана в том, что он старается встроить "социальный аспект" в традиционное представление об эпистемологии, сохранив при этом все индивидуалистические принципы функционирования эпистемологии.
- Напротив, Фуллер говорит о полном пересмотре традиционной эпистемологии, например, он критикует аналитический подход, говоря о том, что он больше подходит для адроидов, потому что не учитывает человеческие свойства в процессе производства знания. Фуллер говорит о том, что индивидуальность не может быть изначально фундаментальным принципом знания, поскольку знание изначально является социальной концепцией - эпистемические стандарты возникают в процессе социального взаимодействия. Эпистемология должна изучать социальное конструирования знания.
## Questions and answers
- how extended knowledge, the result of combining active externalism from contemporary philosophy of mind with contemporary epistemology, can offer an alternative conception of the future of social epistemology? (стр. 105)
- what counts as epistemology in general? (стр. 106)
- what counts as "real epistemology"? Goldman’s method for testing whether some approach counts as real epistemology comes in two steps. (стр. 106)
	- He begins by putting forward what he considers to be the “central features of epistemology ‘as we know it’ (in the analytic fashion).” (стр. 106)
		- "the epistemic agents of traditional epistemology are exclusively individuals.” (стр. 107)
		- epistemology is a normative domain focusing on evaluative concepts such as justifiedness, rationality, and knowledge; (стр.107)
		- those evaluative concepts are not conventional or relativistic; (стр. 107)
		- knowledge and justification either entail or are closely related to truth; (стр. 107)
		- truth is objective and mind independent; (стр. 107)
		- the central task of traditional epistemology is the critical examination of doxastic decision making. (стр. 107)
	- the second general approach to social epistemology, which Goldman further subdivides into ‘preservationism’ and ‘expansionism’. (стр. 107)
		- Preservationist social epistemology studies themes such as testimonial knowledge and peer disagreement by focusing primarily on the individual epistemic agent, thereby preserving the core assumptions of analytic epistemology. (стр. 107)
		- [Expansionist social epistemology] expands its interests to topics such as the epistemic properties of group doxastic agents and the influence of social systems and their policies on epistemic outcomes, possibly distancing itself from some of the core individualistic assumptions of mainstream epistemology. (стр. 107)
- Что такое "доксатический"? Этим словом обозначаются те сущности, которые в нашей голове основаны на вере или убеждении.
- So how can we introduce active externalism within epistemology?
	- The most obvious starting point is the approach of process reliabilism, due to its central focus on the reliability of epistemic mechanisms, methods, and processes: *S knows that p, if and only if S’s true belief is the product of a reliable belief-forming process*. Суть подхода заключается в том, что знание о чем-то является истинным, только если оно было предоставлено надежным источником [[Extended Knowledge and Social Epistemology#^575b73]].
	- Апгрейд предыдущего подхода - ability intuition on knowlage. Он говорит о том, что надежным источником может быть признан только когнитивный процесс. Это принцип, который лежит в основании **virtue reliebilism**. **Virtue reliabilism** ставит в центр познания агента, который имеет когнитивные способности [[Extended Knowledge and Social Epistemology#^c49975]].

> Virtue reliabilists hold that a belief-forming process counts as knowledge-conducive only if it is one of the reliable processes that make up or have been integrated into the agent’s cognitive character. (стр. 112)
^c49975

> If S knows that p, then S’s true belief that p is the product of a reliable belief-forming process, which is appropriately integrated within S’s cognitive character such that her cognitive success is to a significant degree creditable to her cognitive agency (Pritchard 2010b, 136-7). (стр. 112)
^f67620

> COGAweak accommodates the ability intuition on knowledge by claiming that a reliable belief-forming process is knowledge-conducive only if it has been appropriately integrated within the agent’s cognitive character. (стр. 113)

> There is nothing in the formulation of COGAweak or in the concepts involved thereof that restricts knowledge-conducive cognitive abilities to those cognitive processes within the agent’s head. (стр. 113)

> The idea of a cognitive character that may consist of “acquired methods of inquiry including those involving highly specialized training or even advanced technology” seems to be compatible with, or even prefigure, the hypothesis of extended cognition. (стр. 113)

> Both views [COGAweak and extended cognition] state that in order for a process to count as a cognitive ability it must be (a) reliable (i.e., not subject to critical scrutiny), (b) one of the agent’s dispositions (such that it will be typically invoked), and (c) integrated within the rest of the agent’s cognitive system/character (such that it will be easily accessible as if it were part of the agent’s organismic cognitive apparatus). (стр. 113)

> The requirement for a belief-forming process to be integrated into the agent’s cognitive character is a straightforward answer to Fuller’s worry that mere process reliabilism pays no attention to our sentient natures. (стр. 113)

> In contrast to process reliabilism, virtue reliabilism is not interested in isolated automatic processes that reliably generate true beliefs, but in the interwound totality of them that constitutes the agent’s cognitive character. (стр. 113)

> First, we can claim that our knowledge-conducive cognitive characters may, and indeed many times do, extend beyond our organismic cognitive faculties. (стр. 113)

> the subject’s belief satisfies COGAweak, since his believing the truth is significantly creditable to his cognitive agency (i.e., his organismic cognitive apparatus). (стр. 114)

> We can explain how it is possible to acquire knowledge on the basis of artifacts while remaining fast to the guiding intuition that knowledge is belief that is true in virtue of cognitive ability (viz., the ability intuition on knowledge). (стр. 114)
^a4ae6b

> Given that artifacts may be both hardware and software, we finally have the means and the incentive to provide a single account of knowledge that can be universally applied, despite the fact that knowledge can be attained via disparate processes whose (physical) implementation may be entirely unrelated. (стр. 114)

> Such an approach can reveal the partly social nature of many instances of individually produced knowledge. (стр. 114)

> Previously, we noted that when an agent gains knowledge on the basis of an artifact, her cognitive success will be significantly creditable to her cognitive agency on account of her appropriately integrating the artifact within her cognitive character. (стр. 114)
^b2cd0e

> Notice, however, that frequently we will not be able to attribute the rest of the credit to only one single individual, because, in most cases, in order to come up with such reliable belief-forming processes the individual employs similar belief- forming processes or relies on knowledge that has been delivered by other individuals on the basis of further reliable belief-forming processes, and so on. (стр. 114)
^483d27

> By combining COGAweak to the extended cognition hypothesis, we gain a view of knowledge whereby the individual agent can be an advanced epistemic agent only within a given social structure necessary for supplying him with the reliable-belief forming processes that he will later integrate within his cognitive character so as to come to know the truth of some proposition. (стр. 115)

> The second interesting ramification we get by combining virtue reliabilism with active externalism — and, in particular, with the hypothesis of distributed cognition — is that we can account for epistemic group agents. (стр. 115)

> These are groups of individuals who exist and gain knowledge in virtue of a shared common cognitive character that primarily consists of a distributed cognitive ability. (стр. 115)
^b6460b

> Is not reducible to the cognitive abilities possessed by the individual members, thereby allowing us to speak of a group agent in itself. (стр. 115)

> Even though the knowledge-conducive belief-forming process consists of several experts and their experimental devices engaging in mutual (socio-epistemic) interactions, the collective cognitive success of believing the truth of some (scientific) proposition will still be significantly creditable to the group’s cognitive agency (i.e., the set of the organismic cognitive faculties of its individual members). (стр. 115)

> Rather than understanding knowledge as an individualistic phenomenon (where the cognitive individual is confined within the cranium of the subject), we instead get a conception of knowledge which can be extended in terms of the subject’s interactions with epistemic artifacts and which can also be distributed in terms of her social epistemic interactions. (стр. 115)

> Contemporary epistemology, therefore, has the resources to address a variety of disparate intuitions about knowledge without departing radically from the analytic tradition. (стр. 116)
