## Questions and answers
- Большая часть литературы об ИИ неверно интерпретирует содержание предмета.
- Исследователи слишком обрадовались тому как хорошо экстернализм интерпретирует ИИ, поэтому упустили влияние антропоцентрических предрассудков на описание природы ИИ.
- Люди подходят к проблемам содержания ИИ с неоправданно узким представлением о том как это содержание может быть определено. С другой стороны другие люди выбирают слишком узкую выборку носителей содержания, чтобы на их примере это содержание рассматривать. [[De-Anthropocentrized Externalism#^a34771]]
- Три варианта подхода к интерпретации сложных систем искусственного интеллекта (все три являются с точки зрения автора неверными): [[De-Anthropocentrized Externalism#^83bb51]]
- Точно так же как мы пытаемся расчленить продуктивную деятельность ИИ с помощью разложения всего механизма на составные части мы могли бы попробовать сделать и с человеческим организмом: попробовать вычислить на основании функционала разных частей мозга почему мы мыслим именно так, а не иначе. [[De-Anthropocentrized Externalism#^1059df]]

## Quotes
> That claim contains a bit of bad news: much of the work being done on interpretability of artificial intelligence, we think, centres around an incorrect picture of how content is determined. (стр. 59)

> But it also contains a bit of good news: work on the determination of content in philosophy provides a better externalist picture of how content is determined and sophisticated tools for developing that picture. (стр. 59)

> That’s because consideration of some of the specific details about AI systems reveals that the externalist accounts that philosophers have developed contain crippling anthropocentric biases that make them unsuitable for use on nonhuman cases like AI systems. (стр. 59)

> On one side, people have been approaching problems of AI content with an unnecessarily narrow picture of how contents might be determined. On the other side, people have been thinking about content determination using an unnecessarily narrow range of cases of content bearers. (стр. 60)
^a34771

> Computational structure of machine learning neural networks makes it incredibly difficult to produce this kind of story grounding content in the programming/computational details. (стр. 61)

> (1) Some people get tempted into scepticism about content, thinking that there’s no way to tell a content-­level story on the basis of such obscure computational mechanisms. (2) Some people get tempted into thinking that AI systems must have wildly alien contents, representing (perhaps) massively disjunctive properties that are computationally tracked by the details of the neural network, but which can’t be expressed or comprehended by humans. (3) Some people think that humanly comprehensible contents can be extracted from the computational details of the neural network, but that sophisticated tools of computational intervention are needed to figure out how specific contents are grounded in specific portions of specific neural networks. (Example: the rapidly expanding body of work on feature visualization tools.) (стр. 62)
^83bb51

> Our first central lesson for this book is that all [имеются в виду три принципа рассмотрения природы ИИ, когда LLM или другая система выдает продуктивный результат] of the above is the wrong way to think about the problem of AI content. (стр. 62)

> AI content is a problem at the level of environmental and sociological detail. (стр. 62)

> This is not the first time we’ve encountered the problem of assigning content to systems whose computational details are enormously, and perhaps incomprehensibly, complex. (стр. 62)

> It’s possible to take the computational perspective on content determination for people. To take this perspective involves thinking that what a person means by the sentences they utter, or what the contents of their beliefs are, or what features of the world they are representing in perception, are determined by the computational details of their neuroanatomy. (стр. 63)

> We can extract a research program: consider the computational structure of various parts of human brains at various levels of abstraction, and try to determine which of those computational structures manage to represent and what they represent. (стр. 63)
^1059df

> An important alternative perspective on content determination which argues that that entire research program is a mistake. Externalist views hold that the problem of content determination for people isn’t a computational problem; it’s an environmental and sociological problem. (стр. 63)
